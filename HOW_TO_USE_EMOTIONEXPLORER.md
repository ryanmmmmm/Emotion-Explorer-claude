# How to Use Emotion Explorer

## Overview
Emotion Explorer is an interactive emotional intelligence game with **two distinct experiences** based on age:
- **Teen Version (Ages 12-18)**: Adventure-themed with fantasy names and quest narrative
- **Adult Version (Ages 19+)**: Straightforward therapeutic language with clinical names

## Module Details

### Module 1: Awakening Circle / Mood Meter
**What You Do:**
- Use the intensity slider to rate your emotion from 0-10
- Each level shows a description (e.g., "Mild", "Moderate", "Intense")
- Write where you feel this emotion in your body

**Purpose:** Establish baseline emotional awareness

**Example:** "How intense is your feeling? I am feeling this in a very intense way (8/10). I feel butterflies in my stomach."

---

### Module 2: Memory Constellation / Map Your Feelings
**What You Do:**
- Write words, phrases, scenes, names, or times associated with this feeling
- Free association - let your mind wander
- Connect memories to current emotion

**Purpose:** Identify emotional patterns and triggers

**Example:** "CONCERNED â†’ summer camp 2019, phone call from mom, dark basement, that conversation with Sarah, feeling alone"

---

### Module 3: Reflection Mirror / Body Language
**What You Do:**
- Identify **where** in your body you feel this emotion
- Describe **what it feels like** (physical sensations)
- If that body part could **talk**, what would it say?
- If it could **ask** for something, what would it ask?
- What **action** would it take?

**Purpose:** Connect emotional experience to physical embodiment

**Example Prompts:**
- "Tightness in my chest"
- "It feels like a heavy weight pressing down"
- "My chest would say: 'I'm scared of being hurt again'"
- "It would ask for: 'safety and reassurance'"
- "It would take action: reach out for support"

---

### Module 4: Speaking Stone / Letter Writing
**What You Do:**
- Write a detailed letter expressing your emotional experience
- No filters or editing - raw expression
- Describe the situation, feelings, and thoughts

**Purpose:** Process emotions through expressive writing

**Example:** "Dear [Person/Self], I need to express how I'm feeling about..."

---

### Module 5: Mirror Portal / Reverse Letter Writing
**What You Do:**
- Write from a different perspective:
  - From someone else involved in the situation
  - From your future self looking back
  - From a compassionate observer

**Purpose:** Develop cognitive flexibility and empathy

**Example:** "Dear [Your Name], Looking back on this from the future, I want you to know..."

---

### Module 6: Cathartic Falls / Feelings Journal
**What You Do:**
- Free-form journaling about your emotional experience
- No structure - let thoughts flow naturally
- Explore whatever comes up without judgment

**Purpose:** Deepen self-understanding through reflection

---

### Module 7: Emotional Compass / Trajectories
**What You Do:**
- Map the **past**: Where did this emotion come from?
- Map the **present**: Where is it now?
- Map the **future**: Where might it be going?
- Trace the emotional timeline

**Purpose:** Understand emotional patterns over time

**Example:**
- Past: "This started when I lost my job last month"
- Present: "Now it shows up whenever I think about finances"
- Future: "It might ease once I find new work and rebuild confidence"

---

### Module 8: Wisdom Tree / Spiritual Awakening
**What You Do:**
- Explore deeper meaning: What is this emotion teaching you?
- Connect to values: How does it relate to what matters most?
- Find purpose: What growth opportunity does it offer?

**Purpose:** Find transcendent meaning in emotional experience

**Example:** "This fear is teaching me to value my relationships. It's showing me what I don't want to lose."

---

### Module 9: Ripple Pool / Intentions and Next Steps
**What You Do:**
- Set clear intentions for working with this emotion
- Identify 3-5 specific actions you will take
- Create concrete next steps

**Purpose:** Bridge awareness to behavior change

**Example Actions:**
- "Call my friend this week for support"
- "Practice the breathing exercise daily"
- "Journal for 10 minutes each morning"

## Tips for Best Experience

### For Teens:
- Embrace the adventure theme - you're on a quest!
- Your companion guide offers wisdom throughout
- Earn "emotional powers" by completing modules
- Use your avatar to represent your journey

### For Adults:
- Treat this as a therapeutic tool
- Be honest and thorough in responses
- Use clinical insights provided
- Apply evidence-based techniques in daily life

### General:
- **Take your time** - there's no rush
- **Be honest** - only you see your responses
- **Write freely** - more detail = more insight
- **Complete all 9 modules** for maximum benefit
- **Return regularly** to explore different emotions
- **Your progress is saved** - pick up where you left off

## Navigation

- **Hub**: Central area where you select emotions
- **Companion**: Guide offering support (click avatar for chat)
- **Module Progress**: See which modules completed
- **Back to Hub**: Return anytime between modules
